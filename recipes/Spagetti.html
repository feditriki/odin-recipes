Description

Spaghetti Carbonara is a traditional Roman pasta dish made with simple ingredients that deliver incredible flavor. It combines pasta with crispy pancetta, eggs, Parmesan cheese, and black pepper to create a rich, creamy sauce without any cream.

The key to a perfect Carbonara is timing and heat control â€” you cook the eggs gently using the residual heat from the pasta to make a silky, glossy sauce. It's quick, comforting, and perfect for any pasta lover.

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 150g pancetta or bacon, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 50g grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 clove garlic (optional)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Steps

  1. Cook the pasta

    2. Boil spaghetti in salted water until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining.

  2. Prepare pancetta

    3. In a pan, heat olive oil and cook pancetta (and garlic if using) until crispy. Remove garlic afterward.

  3. Mix eggs and cheese

    4. In a bowl, whisk eggs with grated Parmesan and a generous pinch of black pepper.

  4. Combine everything

    5. Add hot spaghetti to the pancetta, remove from heat, then stir in egg mixture quickly to avoid scrambling. Use pasta water to loosen the sauce if needed.

  5. Serve and enjoy

    6. Plate immediately and top with more Parmesan and pepper.
